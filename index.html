Positive Self Talk

One of the best self care tools is positive self talk. This requires challenging your thoughts about yourself when they are critical. You can ask "Is this true?" and "Is this helpful?" After you answer these questions, practice telling yourself true and positive statements that will help you feel safe and calm. Notice the kind words on the Self Care Bear's tummy!

Mindfulness

Mindfulness is an effective grounding technique that helps to minimize anxiety. My favorite is the 5 senses grounding exercise where you find five things you can see, four things you can touch, three things you can hear, two things you can smell, and one thing you can taste.

Keep Hydrated

Keeping hydrated is a simple and easy self care habit to adopt. Being well-hydrated also improves sleep quality, cognition, and mood. It often helps to have an emotional support water bottle.

Celebrate the Wins

A fun motivational skill is celebrating even the small wins. This will trigger feelings of joy and pride which will give you the energy to continue to pursue the small steps that will eventually lead to accomplishing bigger goals. This is especially helpful when learning to code, you deserve to be celebrated every step of the way!

Me Time

Regular, uninterrupted me time helps our busy brains to unplug and unwind. In doing so we relieve stress which helps us sleep better, gives us more control over our moods, increases our patience level, and helps us to live in the moment.

Bubble Bath

A hot bath has been proven to stimulate proper blood flow in your body. Bubble baths lighten your mood and relax your mind. Soaking in a warm bath calms you, plus it is a pure way of minimizing any bad mood or stress you may feel.

Journal It

Expressive writing through journaling can be a powerful way to process stress, trauma, and different emotions.

Medidtate

Meditation pauses the story in your mind long enough for you to remember that your mind is always telling you a story.

Making Smoothies

Did you know that the USDA's suggested fiber intake for lowering your risks of chronic illnesses and increasing your overall health includes a daily serving of 2 cups of fruits and 3 cups of veggies. Smoothies are a delicious way to add more produce into your diet, plus if you freeze the fresh leftovers, it is a relevant way to reduce food waste because they can be turned into smoothies.

Grow Microgreens

Micrigreens are a super food that are easy to grow in any season because they live indoors on your windowsill. They are considered a superfood because they are nutrient dense, and can easily be incorporated into soups, salads and sandwiches. Their health benefits include reducing the risk for chronic illnesses and enhancing your immune system because they are considered a great source of vitamin A, E, C and K, and minerals such as calcium, magnesium, iron, selenium and zinc!

Forest Bathe

Forest bathing has been found to lower blood pressure, heart rate, and levels of harmful hormones — like cortisol, which your body produces when it's stressed. This can help put you in a more calm and relaxed state.

Get Some Good Sleep

Your body releases hormones during sleep that help repair cells and control the body's use of energy.You might sleep 9 to 10 hours a night if your sleep need is this amount or if you have high sleep debt and your body is catching up on lost sleep or recovering from an illness. Sometimes it is difficult to fall asleep, but sleep affirmations help.

Give Yourself Some Grace

Giving yourself grace truly helps when life gets hard. You are doing your best, be kind to yourself. Give yourself a mental health day and get cozy and comfy in a safe space.

Taste the Sunshine

Did you know that plants eat sunshine? They store the sun's energy inside and turn it into sugar. You can be solar charged when you eat fruits and veggies, and if you are mindful while eating them you can actually taste the sunshine.

Wanna Hug?

During a hug, we release oxytocin, a hormone that relaxes us and lowers anxiety. It's often called the cuddle hormone and when it's released during these 20-second hugs it can effectively lower blood pressure and reduce the stress hormone norepinephrine. The long and short? Good, long hugs are good for your heart!
